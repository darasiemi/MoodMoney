## Overview

This review examined existing research on the relationship between money and mental health, including the analytical methods used in the field. To ensure a structured and reliable review process, the PRISMA-ScR framework for scoping reviews was applied. Relevant studies were identified through searches across health, technology, and multidisciplinary academic databases.

The research questions underlying the review are: 

- What financial and mental health variables have been examined? 
- What data sources have been used to measure financial factors and mental health?
- What analytical approaches have been used to examine mental health-finance relationships?

## Results

The review found that most studies focused on the impact of financial difficulty on mental health using self-reported data and traditional statistical methods, with limited use of objective financial data or machine learning approaches. The findings highlight opportunities to leverage objective financial data and advanced analytical methods to improve understanding of the bidirectional relationship between financial behaviour and mental health and support the development of targeted interventions.
